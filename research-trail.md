# Research Trail

**Tools used:** Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini

**Queries run:**

I used Claude to search for real-world case studies matching DeepThought's core argument — a company that solved the structural conflict between running today's operations and driving tomorrow's growth, through a dedicated team and a defined protocol rather than motivation or consulting advice.

Initial queries explored mid-market manufacturing turnarounds and companies that had structurally separated improvement from operations. Claude surfaced three candidate case studies: Amazon's Single-Threaded Owner model, Danaher at scale, and the Jake Brake origin story specifically. Jake Brake was selected as the backbone because it matched the brief's audience most closely — a single struggling manufacturing plant with a named owner, a narrow mandate, and a mechanism that maps directly to DeepThought's six conditions.

Subsequent queries focused on the specific mechanism: how Koenigsaecker's mandate was structured, what the Shingijutsu engagement looked like week-to-week, and what quantifiable outcomes were documented.

**How the case study was verified:**

Mechanism details were traced to two primary firsthand sources:

- George Koenigsaecker and Art Byrne, *"Ask Art: What Was Danaher Like in the Early Days of Lean?"* — Lean Enterprise Institute, September 2020
- *"Danaher's Instruments of Change"* — Strategy+Business, February 2016 (executive roundtable featuring Koenigsaecker directly)

The earnings per share growth figure (10,000% from 1990–2023) was cross-checked against Quartr Insights' documented analysis of Danaher's business system history.

Where specific numbers — particularly plant-level revenue figures for Jake Brake — were not findable in primary sources, they were omitted and the gap disclosed in the footnotes rather than estimated. This was a deliberate guardrail against unverifiable AI-generated data.

The draft was also independently verified multiple times using ChatGPT and Gemini for argument structure and sourcing before final submission.
